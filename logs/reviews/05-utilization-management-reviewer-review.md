**Reviewer:** utilization-management-reviewer
**Artifact:** `whitepaper/claim-cost-containment-v2-draft.md`
**Date:** 2026-05-22
**Verdict:** ACCEPT WITH REVISIONS

## What works

- §2.5 correctly identifies the PA list as the central design choice.
- "Annual sunset on services with >95% approval rate" is correct and underused industry-wide.
- Denial defensibility framed procedurally (criteria, rationale, peer-to-peer, member rights, jurisdictional deadlines).

## Findings

1. **Gold-carding missing.** This is a major operational lever — providers with high historical approval rates get PA waived. Reduces provider abrasion at zero savings loss. Many state laws (TX, MI, others) now mandate gold-carding consideration. The paper should not omit it.
2. **Avoided-cost measurement methodology is too brief.** The paper says nothing about matched-cohort validation. The agent spec correctly identifies that "denied dollars × 100%" is vendor-marketing math. The white paper should at least allude to this — readers underestimate how flimsy UM savings claims are without matched-cohort design.
3. **Provider abrasion as a cost is missing.** PA-heavy programs lose network providers, generate member complaints, and trigger regulator inquiry. Abrasion is a measurable cost. The paper treats UM as upside-only; it isn't.
4. **MHPAEA NQTL interaction not named here.** PA list disproportionately on BH services is the most-litigated parity failure. The paper names parity in §2.12 but does not link §2.5 to it. UM and parity must be designed together.

## Required edits

- Add "gold-carding" to §2.5 description.
- Add a phrase on matched-cohort UM measurement (link to §3 measurement contract).
- One sentence on provider abrasion as a measurable cost.
- Cross-link §2.5 ↔ §2.12 on NQTL parity.

## Out of scope

State-by-state UR statute matrix (esp. WC) — belongs in service-specific deliverable.
