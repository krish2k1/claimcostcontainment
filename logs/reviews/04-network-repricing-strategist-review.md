# Review — network-repricing-strategist

**Reviewer:** network-repricing-strategist
**Artifact:** `whitepaper/claim-cost-containment-v2-draft.md`
**Date:** 2026-05-22
**Verdict:** ACCEPT WITH MINOR REVISIONS

## What works

- §2.4 waterfall is correctly ordered: direct → primary → wrap → RBP → OON → default.
- Exclusivity + lineage discipline is named — this is the right hill to die on and it is rare to see it stated this directly.
- NSA QPA + IDR + state-OON-statute interplay is named.

## Findings

1. **PPO discount illusion is missing.** The paper does not warn that a "55% PPO discount" tells you nothing without the Medicare-multiple context. Two networks both quoting "55%" can produce paid amounts that differ by 80%+ on the same service. The paper's audience will read PPO discount % as a comparable metric; it is not.
2. **RBP member-defense is named but not explained.** RBP without a balance-bill defense vendor and member legal-defense fund will produce member complaints, employer reputation damage, and litigation. The paper should name member-defense as a non-negotiable component of any RBP program, not an optional enhancement.
3. **Direct contracts and COE are listed as #1 in the waterfall but their economics aren't explained.** Direct contracts often outperform PPO by 15–35% on price *and* are stronger on quality. A reader looking at the waterfall might assume "direct contracts" is a niche move; the paper should name it as the *strategic* lever, with PPO as the *coverage-of-leakage* lever.
4. **NSA QPA documentation is the #1 IDR loss factor.** The paper should note that the dispute is usually won or lost on the QPA derivation file, not on the price.

## Required edits

- Sentence on PPO-discount-vs-Medicare-multiple in §2.4.
- Sentence elevating direct / COE as the strategic lever.
- Sentence on QPA documentation being the IDR-determining artifact.

## Out of scope

State-by-state OON statute matrix; transparency-rule (MRF / TiC) implementation — service-line briefing material.
