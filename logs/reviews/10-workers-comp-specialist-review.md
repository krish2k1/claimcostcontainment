**Verdict:** ACCEPT WITH REVISIONS

## What works

- §2.10 correctly states WC is not group health with a different fee schedule. This is the single most important framing in any cross-LoB cost-containment paper and it is too often missing.
- Compensability / causation / MMI / indemnity / MSA / RTW named.
- "Every other lever applies differently in WC" is correct.

## Findings

1. **Indemnity is the larger half of WC severity for most blocks.** The paper is medical-cost-containment-flavored throughout, but WC indemnity (TTD / TPD / PPD / PTD) frequently exceeds medical on lost-time claims. A paper promising to cover all services must name indemnity as a service line *within* WC, not just as a sub-bullet. Worth a paragraph.
2. **First-touch latency to medical care.** The single highest-leverage WC intervention is the first 4–24 hours after injury report — telephonic nurse triage, designated-provider routing, transitional duty plan. This is missing from §2.10 and from §2.11.
3. **State MPN / preferred-provider rules.** Some states (CA, TX, FL, PA) require certified networks or MPNs and have specific OON penalty structures. The paper's NSA discussion is group-health-only; a WC reader needs to know NSA does not apply, but state MPN rules do.
4. **Presumption statutes.** Firefighter cancer, COVID for healthcare workers — many states have presumption statutes that override standard causation analysis. Not naming these in §2.10 leaves a major compensability blind spot.
5. **Medical-only vs lost-time triage.** This distinction drives the entire WC severity story. A medical-only claim becoming lost-time is almost always a preventable medical-management failure. Worth one sentence.

## Required edits

- Indemnity layer paragraph in §2.10.
- First-touch latency mention (§2.10 or cross-link to §2.11).
- One sentence on state MPN rules.
- One sentence on presumption statutes.
- One sentence on medical-only vs lost-time as the triage point.

## Out of scope

State-by-state fee schedule matrix; MSA submission threshold dollar values (changes); jurisdictional UR statute table — service-line deliverables.
