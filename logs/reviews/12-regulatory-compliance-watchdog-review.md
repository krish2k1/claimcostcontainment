## Findings

1. **NQTL is named, but the practical comparative-analysis obligation is not.** Under MHPAEA and DOL/HHS enforcement guidance, plans must produce a documented **comparative analysis** demonstrating BH/SUD NQTLs are no more restrictive than M/S in design *or application*. Plans without this document on the shelf are exposed at first audit request. The paper should make this concrete.
2. **ERISA preemption nuance missing.** "ERISA preempts most state law for self-funded plans" is the typical takeaway, but the *insurance saving clause*, the *deemer clause*, and the carve-outs (criminal, generally-applicable, federal layer — HIPAA, ACA, NSA, MHPAEA) are the operational truth. A senior reader will notice the gap. One paragraph.
3. **CAA 2021 gag-clause attestation.** Plans are required to file an annual attestation that their service-provider contracts do not contain gag clauses on price/quality data. This is enforced and most TPAs / vendors had to renegotiate. Worth a sentence given the audience.
4. **HIPAA + BAA for cost-containment vendors.** Every vendor named in this paper handles PHI. The paper does not mention BAAs. Self-funded employers commonly miss BAAs with bill-review and subro vendors. Worth a line.
5. **State PBM regulation is fast-moving.** The paper says it but should note "rapidly evolving"; copay accumulator restrictions, MAC transparency, PBM-licensing requirements have changed in 30+ states in the last 24 months.

## Required edits

- Concrete mention of MHPAEA comparative-analysis document.
- One paragraph on ERISA preemption mechanics (saving clause, deemer clause, carve-outs).
- Gag-clause attestation line.
- BAA requirement for cost-containment vendors.
- "Rapidly evolving" hedge on state PBM regulation.
