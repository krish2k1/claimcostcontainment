# Review — care-management-steerage-strategist

**Reviewer:** care-management-steerage-strategist
**Artifact:** `whitepaper/claim-cost-containment-v2-draft.md`
**Date:** 2026-05-22
**Verdict:** ACCEPT WITH REVISIONS

## What works

- §2.11 correctly identifies upstream-of-claim as the highest-leverage intervention.
- COE benefit-design point (waived cost-share, travel benefits) named explicitly.
- BH integration on top 5% of high-cost claimants named.

## Findings

1. **Trajectory-model action-gate discipline is missing.** A model whose output sits in a dashboard with no defined action is *cost*, not *capability*. The paper should explicitly state that every analytics output must connect to a downstream clinical action. This is the dashboard-theater problem.
2. **Member experience is treated as a soft constraint; it is a hard one.** Programs that save money and generate disenrollment / complaint volume are not wins. The paper says this implicitly in §4 but the care-management section should name member-experience as a measurable constraint, not a vibe.
3. **Engagement bias / matched-cohort discipline missing.** "Engaged member vs unengaged member" comparisons are confounded by selection. The paper claims rigour on UM measurement (§3) but does not extend the same discipline to care management. Vendors routinely sell unmatched ROI in this space.
4. **Site-of-care steerage cross-link.** §2.11 names "site-of-care optimization" but the meatiest cross-line example (infusibles, §2.8) is not linked. Worth a one-line cross-reference.
5. **Maternity / NICU diversion magnitude understated.** A single NICU diversion can be $100K–$500K+. For a small or mid-size self-funded employer, this is the biggest single-event lever in the entire stack. Worth a line.

## Required edits

- Add "every model output must connect to a defined clinical action" line.
- Promote "member experience is a measurable constraint" from soft to hard.
- Add matched-cohort discipline for CM measurement (link to §3).
- Cross-link §2.11 site-of-care ↔ §2.8 specialty Rx.
- Concrete NICU-diversion magnitude.

## Out of scope

Vendor scoring methodology; navigation-program engagement design — service-line deliverables.
