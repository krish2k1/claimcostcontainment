# Claim Cost Containment

### A Financial and Operating Framework for Payers, TPAs, Carriers, and Self-Insured Employers

*Mahathi Infotech — White Paper · May 2026 · v4 (3-page)*

---

## Executive Summary

Cost containment is an exercise in moving loss ratios. Most programs fail not because the levers are unknown but because their savings are computed against unstable baselines, in vendor-marketing arithmetic, under incentives that point participants away from the structural fix. This paper presents the financial lens, the twelve service lines that together constitute a credible cost-containment capability, the measurement contract every program must answer in writing, and the recurring failure modes to design against.

A warning that sets the tone: **accelerating or deferring paid losses is not the same as reducing ultimate incurred losses.** Only the *ultimate-incurred* difference is real economic savings, and only the program designed against that number survives audit.

## 1. The Financial Lens

| Ratio | Formula | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Loss Ratio (LR)** | Incurred Losses ÷ Earned Premium | Headline underwriting performance |
| **Medical Loss Ratio (MLR), ACA** | (Incurred Claims + Quality Activities) ÷ (Premium − Taxes/Fees), 3-yr credibility-adjusted | Triggers rebates below 85% large group / 80% small group & individual; small blocks (under ~1,000 life-years) fall below the credibility threshold entirely |
| **LAE Ratio** | Loss Adjustment Expenses ÷ Earned Premium | Split into **ALAE** (claim-tied: bill review, UR, case management, defense) and **ULAE** (claims-dept overhead). Cost-containment vendor fees are almost entirely ALAE |
| **Expense Ratio** | Underwriting Expenses ÷ Premium | Commission, admin, premium tax |
| **Combined Ratio** | LR + LAE + Expense | < 100% = underwriting profit |
| **Operating Ratio** | Combined − Net Investment Income Ratio | True economic profitability |
| **Burning Cost** | Ceded Losses ÷ Subject Premium | Prices excess (reinsurance) layers |
| **Pure Premium** | Losses ÷ Exposure Unit | PMPM (group health, per member-month); per-$100-of-payroll (workers' compensation) |

Self-insured employers do not have premium; they have a *budget rate* or *premium-equivalent*, and the ACA MLR rebate framework does not apply to them. Two further distinctions are routinely blurred. **Calendar-year (CY)** loss ratios mix accident periods and reserve development; only **accident-year (AY)** ratios, restated to the year of injury or onset, judge a cost-containment program. **Paid** loss ratios can be flattered by acceleration or deferral; only **ultimate-incurred** is real savings.

**Trend decomposition.** Total trend ≈ unit-cost × utilization × mix shift × buy-down/up × contract step-up. Industry-survey ranges in the current cycle (Mercer, KFF, Milliman MGTR) typically resolve to unit cost +3 to +4%, utilization +1 to +2%, specialty Rx mix +2 to +4%, contract step-up around +1%. Decomposition determines which lever to pull: unit-cost-driven blocks need network and repricing; utilization-driven blocks need UM and steerage; mix-driven blocks need pharmacy and site-of-care strategy.

**Reserving and the savings baseline.** Every savings claim rests on a reserve view. The mature program triangulates ultimate-incurred losses (chain-ladder paid and incurred, Bornhuetter-Ferguson, Cape Cod, or GLM-based trend); on-levels historical losses to current rate and benefit level; credibility-weights against an industry complement for mid- and small-size blocks; distinguishes pure **IBNR** (events not yet reported) from **IBNER** (development on known claims — often the larger of the two in mature blocks); and treats every program launch as a *process change* requiring a documented method adjustment in the launch cohort.

## 2. The Service Catalog

Twelve service lines, each summarized at its irreducible core.

**2.1 Loss-Ratio & Financial Performance.** Translates every other lever into AY-restated, ALAE-attributed, PMPM/PEPM, CFO-facing language. Without it, savings are anecdotes.

**2.2 Actuarial & Reserving.** Produces the ultimate-incurred view, selects trend, manages credibility-weighted blends, and sizes specific and aggregate stop-loss attachments. Without it, savings rest on an unstable baseline.

**2.3 Stop-Loss & Reinsurance.** ISL (specific) and ASL (aggregate) attachment, lasers, run-in/out alignment, recovery filing. The single most expensive failure mode in cost containment is the missed 50%-of-attachment notification. Stop-loss carriers may not credit RBP or out-of-network (OON) negotiation savings as a "paid amount," so repricing choice must align with the policy savings clause.

**2.4 Network & Repricing.** Operates the repricing waterfall — direct contracts and Centers of Excellence (strategic), then primary PPO, wrap, **Reference-Based Pricing (RBP)**, OON negotiation, plan default — with *exclusivity* (one method per claim, never stacked) and *lineage* (per-claim audit trail). A "55% PPO discount" is meaningless without the Medicare multiple. RBP requires member balance-bill defense. Under the **No Surprises Act (NSA)**, **Independent Dispute Resolution (IDR)** outcomes hinge on the **Qualifying Payment Amount (QPA)** documentation file, not on the price.

**2.5 Utilization Management.** Prospective, concurrent, retrospective review against MCG, InterQual, or ODG (WC). The Prior Authorization (PA) list is the central design choice — short, evidence-graded, with annual sunset on services above 95% approval. *Gold-carding* (waiving PA for high-approval providers) reduces abrasion at zero savings cost. Denial is the wrong tool when steerage is the right tool; avoided-cost claims require matched-cohort validation.

**2.6 Payment Integrity.** Duplicate detection, Coordination of Benefits (COB), eligibility, fee-schedule and contract-term application, Diagnosis-Related Group (DRG) validation, itemized bill review. The mature program *shifts left* — converting recurring post-pay recoveries into pre-pay edits, where dollars recover at ~100% versus 25–60% post-pay. A plan that leaves a known recurring leak in post-pay because the recovery vendor is paid on percent-of-savings is structurally betraying itself. DRG validation requires physician-reviewer documentation.

**2.7 Subrogation, TPL & Recovery.** Subrogation, third-party liability, **Medicare Secondary Payer (MSP) / Section 111**, COB, overpayment, and stop-loss recovery. Yield is gated by *trigger detection* (ICD-10 mechanism-of-injury codes, pharmacy-trauma scans) and by statute-of-limitations discipline — a barred claim is $0. Under 42 USC §1395y(b)(3)(A), missed primary-payer obligations are subject to *double damages*. ERISA recovery depends on plan-document language strength.

**2.8 Pharmacy & PBM.** Specialty drugs now account for roughly 50% of Rx spend on under 2% of utilization. Diagnoses PBM contract structure (spread versus pass-through; AWP / WAC / NADAC / MAC mechanics; rebate guarantee versus pass-through), 340B exposure, and J-code / medical-benefit audit. Effective-rate guarantees are routinely gamed via MAC manipulation. Site-of-care steerage for infusibles is a 20–40% lever.

**2.9 Fraud, Waste & Abuse / SIU.** Triages fraud, abuse, waste, and error through outlier profiling, network-graph signals, and sequence patterns. Intent or recklessness inference distinguishes fraud from billing error. For Medicare Advantage and Medicaid MCO plans, **False Claims Act (FCA) and qui tam** exposure is the largest dollar-risk: failing to detect or report fraud is itself FCA-actionable.

**2.10 Workers' Compensation.** WC is not group health with a different fee schedule; it is a no-fault statutory scheme with state-specific compensability, causation, Maximum Medical Improvement (MMI), and a settlement architecture (Compromise & Release, stipulated awards) that group health does not have. **Indemnity** — Temporary Total / Partial, Permanent Partial / Total — is a service line in its own right; on lost-time claims it frequently exceeds medical. The 4-to-24-hour first-touch window after injury report drives downstream outcome more than any other intervention.

**2.11 Care Management & Steerage.** Nurse case management, disease management, behavioral health integration, maternity / NICU diversion, Centers of Excellence (COE) steerage, member navigation, site-of-care optimization. The highest-leverage intervention is *upstream of the claim*. COE requires waived-cost-share benefit design or utilization stays trivially low. Every model output must connect to a defined clinical action; matched-cohort validation is mandatory.

**2.12 Regulatory & Compliance.** Designed in, not audited in. Federal: ERISA, ACA MLR and Essential Health Benefits, **Mental Health Parity Act (MHPAEA)** — including the comparative-analysis document required for **non-quantitative treatment limitations (NQTL)** — NSA, HIPAA plus the Business Associate Agreement that attaches to every cost-containment vendor handling PHI, CMS MSP / Section 111, Anti-Kickback / Stark / FCA, CAA 2021 transparency. State: prompt-pay, balance-bill (where not preempted), rapidly evolving Pharmacy Benefit Manager (PBM) law, WC fee schedules and Utilization Review (UR) statutes.

**Service-line × compliance-gate matrix:**

| Service line | Dominant compliance gates |
|---|---|
| §2.4 Network | NSA QPA / IDR; AKS / Stark on direct contracts & COE; state OON (fully-insured) |
| §2.5 UM | MHPAEA NQTL; ERISA appeal & external review; state UR timing (WC) |
| §2.6 Payment Integrity | State prompt-pay (clawback windows); provider-appeal due process |
| §2.7 Subrogation | MSP / Section 111; ERISA reimbursement language; state SoL |
| §2.8 Pharmacy | MHPAEA Rx-tier NQTL; state PBM; 340B; white-bagging |
| §2.9 FWA | NAIC anti-fraud / state DOI referral; FCA / qui tam; due process on pre-pay suspension |
| §2.10 WC | State WC fee schedule / UR / MPN / formulary; presumption statutes; MSA / Section 111 |
| §2.11 Care Management | Network adequacy; benefit-design parity; transparency rule |
| All | HIPAA + BAA; CAA gag-clause attestation; broker-compensation disclosure |

## 3. The Measurement Contract

| Question | Default Answer |
|---|---|
| What is the baseline? | The lower of (a) the plan's allowed amount before the lever applied, or (b) a fixed external benchmark (Medicare × factor, current-year fee schedule, prior-paid). Billed charges are not defensible. |
| Paid, incurred, or ultimate? | Ultimate. Paid views are quarterly comfort; only ultimate-incurred movement is real economic savings. |
| Stacked or attributed once? | Attributed once. Every dollar belongs to exactly one lever in the waterfall. |
| Vendor fees? | Reported **net of fees**, with ALAE attribution. Gross figures are vendor marketing. |
| Reporting unit? | PMPM / PEPM (group health), per-claim and per-$100-payroll (WC), AY-restated, against matched prior period. |
| How is UM avoided-cost validated? | Matched-cohort comparison. Raw "denied dollars × 100%" is vendor-marketing math. |
| How is care-management ROI validated? | Matched-cohort comparison. Engagement bias is real. |
| Who audits? | Internal audit or independent third party; sampled-and-extrapolated; at least annually. |

Deviations from these defaults require a documented rationale, signed off by finance and actuarial, *before* the program launches.

## 4. Structural Failure Modes

1. **Misaligned incentives and late detection.** Percent-of-savings (PoS) vendor fees reward inflated baselines and oppose the pre-pay shift; the same vendor that catches leakage post-pay at 25–60% recovery has no incentive to codify it as a pre-pay edit at ~100% avoidance.
2. **Jurisdictional drift.** Group-health logic applied to WC; commercial logic applied to self-funded ERISA; NSA workflows bolted onto preempted state OON statutes. Every lever needs a jurisdictional gate at design time.
3. **Compliance retrofit.** Programs designed against financial KPIs alone fail MHPAEA NQTL audits, miss CAA gag-clause attestations, neglect Business Associate Agreements, and trigger DOL / HHS / state DOI enforcement.
4. **Provider abrasion and defamatory writing.** PA-heavy UM, post-pay clawback campaigns, and FWA artefacts using conclusory rather than conditional language ("consistent with", "warrants further inquiry") generate provider litigation that erases the underlying recovery.

## 5. The Operating-Model Principle

Claim cost containment is not, ultimately, a question of how aggressively a plan denies, reprices, or recovers. It is a question of whether the plan has built an operating model in which the right service line is applied to the right claim at the right time, against a baseline that survives audit, under incentives that point every participant — internal team, vendor, provider, and member — toward the lowest defensible total cost of care.

The blocks of business that outperform on trend over the next decade will share three traits: a *financial lens* that speaks in loss-ratio, ALAE-attributed, AY-restated, ultimate-incurred language; a *service catalog* that covers all twelve lines with explicit coordination contracts between them; and a *measurement contract* signed off by finance, actuarial, and counsel before the first dollar of savings is reported. **A program that cannot answer the measurement contract in writing is not measuring savings — it is invoicing.**
